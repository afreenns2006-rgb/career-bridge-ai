# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Career Bridge AI is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free community for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, political beliefs, race, religion, sex, sexual identity and orientation, socioeconomic status, or any other characteristic.

We are dedicated to fostering an environment where all community members feel valued, heard, and empowered to contribute their best work.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors We Encourage

✅ **Be Respectful**
- Treat all community members with dignity and respect
- Listen actively to others' perspectives
- Acknowledge and celebrate diversity
- Assume good intentions in interactions

✅ **Be Inclusive**
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Make efforts to include underrepresented voices
- Respect different backgrounds and experiences
- Use inclusive language

✅ **Be Collaborative**
- Work together toward common goals
- Share knowledge and help others learn
- Provide constructive feedback
- Celebrate others' contributions and achievements

✅ **Be Professional**
- Use clear and appropriate communication
- Stay focused on the work and ideas
- Avoid unnecessary drama or conflict
- Respect privacy and confidentiality

✅ **Be Responsible**
- Acknowledge and correct mistakes
- Be accountable for your words and actions
- Report problematic behavior appropriately
- Help maintain a safe community

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

❌ **The following behaviors are not tolerated:**

- **Harassment**: Unwanted comments, offensive jokes, slurs, or behavior based on protected characteristics
- **Discrimination**: Treating someone unfairly due to their identity or characteristics
- **Threats or Violence**: Any form of threats, violent language, or physical aggression
- **Abuse**: Verbal attacks, insults, or demeaning behavior toward others
- **Unwanted Advances**: Sexual or romantic advances that are unwelcome or persistent
- **Doxxing**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Harassment of Individuals**: Targeting, stalking, or intimidating community members
- **Spam**: Repetitive, unsolicited promotional content
- **Trolling**: Deliberate attempts to provoke conflict or derail conversations
- **Discrimination**: Exclusion or unfair treatment based on any characteristic

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Project-related communication channels (email, Discord, Slack, forums)
- Project events (conferences, meetups, webinars)
- Official social media accounts
- Any other space representing the project or community

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces, such as:
- Using official social media accounts
- Speaking at conferences or events
- Serving as a project representative
- Contributing to related projects

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**Email**: conduct@careerbridgeai.com

**Report Should Include:**
- Description of the incident
- When and where it occurred
- Who was involved
- Context and impact
- Any supporting evidence

**Important**: All reports are confidential and will be handled professionally.

### Response & Investigation

1. **Acknowledgment**: Reports will be acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: A fair and thorough investigation will be conducted
3. **Confidentiality**: Privacy of all parties will be protected
4. **Communication**: Updates will be provided as appropriate

### Potential Actions

Violations may result in:

- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Required training or counseling
- Temporary suspension from community spaces
- Permanent ban from the project
- Removal of content
- Other appropriate measures

---

## Consequences

### For Contributors

Community members who violate this Code of Conduct may face:

1. **First Violation**
   - Private conversation to clarify expectations
   - Warning about behavior

2. **Second Violation**
   - Formal warning
   - Temporary restrictions on participation

3. **Third Violation**
   - Extended suspension
   - Review for potential permanent removal

### For Maintainers

Maintainers are held to the same standards and may face removal if they:
- Violate the Code of Conduct
- Fail to enforce the Code of Conduct
- Abuse their position
